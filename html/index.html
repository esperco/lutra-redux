Esper is Shutting Down

Esper will shut down on October 13, 2017. If you use Esper for calendar analytics / charting, see below for how to export your data.

After four years of trying to save people time, we’ve decided to shut down Esper. Esper started off as an executive assistant service focused on scheduling but later helped people manage meeting overload by charting how time was spent, creating meeting agendas, and collecting post-meeting ratings. Unfortunately, Esper has not seen the user growth or revenue needed to sustain the product, and we’ve decided to shut things down.

We will be canceling all subscriptions and refunding paying users on a prorated basis as appropriate.

After shutdown, all customer data will be deleted from our systems. This includes all calendar data, payment information, analytics data, and access tokens. We will not sell or provide customer data to any other companies.

We're proud of all the work that everyone at Esper has put into the product over the years, and we regret not being to do more. We continue to believe that excessive and poorly planned meetings remain a very real problem for companies both large and small and believe that there is an opportunity (albeit a difficult one) for anyone who can solve this.

Data Export

If you used Esper's individual charting product, you can export your event tagging data as a CSV file here. If you had access to multiple Esper accounts (e.g. as an executive assistant), you will have to export your data for each account (click on each account in the left sidebar).

If you used Esper for Enterprise, go here to export your data. If you cannot access that page, please check with your team's administrator. If you were a member of multiple Enterprise teams, you will need to export data for each team separately (click on the dropdown in the header to switch teams).
